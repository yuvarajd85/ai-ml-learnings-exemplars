Understanding Agentic RAG

ü§î What is Agentic RAG?

Agentic RAG is like having a smart research assistant instead of a simple search engine. While Traditional RAG just searches and returns results, Agentic RAG can think, plan, and reason about your question before searching.

Imagine asking a librarian for information. A traditional librarian just brings you books matching your keywords. An "agentic" librarian first understands your real need, creates a research plan, checks multiple sources, verifies information, and then gives you a comprehensive answer.

Understanding Corrective RAG (CRAG)

üîç What is Corrective RAG?

Corrective RAG is like having a fact-checker built into your search system. It doesn't just retrieve information - it evaluates and corrects its own results to ensure you get accurate, relevant answers.

Think of it like a GPS that double-checks its directions. If the first route seems wrong (road closed, too long), it automatically recalculates and finds a better path without you asking.

üîÑ LangGraph Architecture

Start Retrieve Relevance Check Score > 0.7? Correct Query Re-retrieve Validate Generate End

‚úÖ Self-Correction Steps

‚Ä¢ Retrieve: Get initial results
‚Ä¢ Evaluate: Score relevance (0-1)
‚Ä¢ Correct: If score < 0.7, modify approach
‚Ä¢ Verify: Ensure final results are accurate

üìä Real Example

CRAG Actions:

‚ú® Why Use Corrective RAG?

Ideal when accuracy is critical and you can't afford wrong answers. It catches and fixes retrieval errors automatically, reducing misinformation and improving user trust.

üéØ What is Adaptive RAG?

Adaptive RAG is like having a smart assistant that automatically chooses the best approach for each question. It adapts its strategy based on query complexity - using simple search for easy questions and advanced reasoning for complex ones.

Like a smart car that switches between eco mode for city driving and sport mode for highways. It automatically adjusts its performance based on what you need, saving resources when possible and using full power when necessary.
